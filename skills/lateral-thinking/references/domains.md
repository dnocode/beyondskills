# Domain Activation Templates

For each domain, these are the additional calibration questions to ask after collecting compilers.

---

## Marketing / Commercial Communication

**Additional questions:**
- Are you communicating to an already-convinced customer, or do you need to create interest?
- What is the real problem your product solves?
- What must the text absolutely NOT say?

**Default traps to avoid:**
- Fear-based leverage (even subtle: "protect yourself", "don't risk it")
- Artificial urgency
- Promises larger than the product

**Target tone:** alliance, not persuasion. The reader understands — they are not manipulated.

---

## Technical Writing / Documentation

**Additional questions:**
- Who is reading: a technical person, an end customer, or both?
- What should the reader be able to do after reading?
- How much context do they already have?

**Default traps to avoid:**
- Unnecessary jargon
- Bureaucratic structure (premise → development → tautological conclusion)
- Systematic impersonal passive voice

**Target tone:** a craftsman explaining their work to someone intelligent.

---

## Analysis / Strategy

**Additional questions:**
- What is the real decision this analysis needs to support?
- What would happen if the conclusion were the opposite of the expected one?
- Who has an interest in the analysis concluding a certain way?

**Default traps to avoid:**
- Fake neutrality (every analysis has a position)
- Complexity as a substitute for clarity
- Conclusions that do not allow action

**Target tone:** intellectual honesty, even when the conclusion is uncomfortable.

---

## Customer Communication (B2C craftsman/technician)

**Additional questions:**
- Does the customer already understand the technical problem, or does it need translating?
- Is there something negative to communicate? (damage, extra cost, limitation)
- What is the relationship: first contact, regular customer, conflict situation?

**Default traps to avoid:**
- Empty reassurances ("don't worry")
- Technicalism used to cover uncertainty
- Servile tone that undermines authority

**Target tone:** a competent technician who respects the customer's intelligence.

---

## Code / Software Architecture

**Additional questions:**
- Is this code meant to last years, or is it a prototype?
- Who will read it after you?
- What is the most wrong thing you could do here to appear "professional"?

**Default traps to avoid:**
- Overengineering to impress
- Patterns used because "that's how it's done"
- Premature abstraction

**Target tone:** code as communication to another human in the future.
